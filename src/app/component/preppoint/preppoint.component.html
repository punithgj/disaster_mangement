Be Prepared: When Disaster Strikes

Knowing what to do before, during, and after a disaster can save lives. Here's your complete guide to preparedness and dealing with emergencies.

How to Prepare for a Disaster

  • Emergency Kit: Assemble a kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, batteries, first-aid supplies, and important documents.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know your local evacuation routes and safe shelters.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather updates and alerts through reliable sources.
  • Home Safety: Secure heavy furniture, unplug appliances, and store hazardous materials safely.
  • Family Communication Plan: Ensure everyone in your family knows how to contact each other during an emergency.

How to Deal with the Situation

  • Stay Calm: Take deep breaths and assess the situation logically. Avoid panic.

  • Help Others: Assist elderly, disabled individuals, and children in reaching safety.
  • Seek Safe Shelter: Move to a designated safe zone or higher ground if necessary.
  • First Aid: Provide basic first aid to injured individuals until professional help arrives.
  • Avoid Risky Areas: Stay away from fallen power lines, flooded areas, or debris.
